# Design Guidelines

## Engage Identity

- Agility should generally have the strongest immediate-damage Engage actions.
- Strength Engage actions should lean toward forceful setup, durability tradeoffs, or sustained physical advantage.
- Intelligence Engage actions should lean toward spells, control, resource setup, and healing over time.
- Exceptions are welcome when an item has a clear cost or build requirement, but the distinction should remain visible across the reward pool.

## Mordrake Visual Bible

Mordrake is an ambiguously ageless legendary hero corrupted by The Black Crown. He should look powerful and heroic before he looks monstrous: broad armored shoulders, noble face, long white-and-grey hair, an ice-cold stare, and visible strain from a troubled mind. Corruption should show around the eyes, temples, veins, and armor seams.

The Black Crown is dark metal with tall jagged spikes and red engravings. It must read immediately as the crown of a dark lord, partly fused to Mordrake rather than simply worn.

His armor is made from blackened metal and consuming dark magic. It should feel impressive, ancient, and ruinous, with smoky seams, ember-red cracks, and red rune engravings. The silhouette should remain consistent across future images: spiked crown, long pale hair, broad dark armor, ragged cloak or mantle, and a defensive warning posture when he first appears.

## Ash Wastelands Creature Looks

The Ash Wastelands should use more than one creature language. Mordrake-aligned guards use blackened metal, ember runes, and ash-cloth silhouettes. Ash Skitterers use cracked shell plates and low insect-lizard shapes.

Cinder Hounds define a living ash beast family: no armor or metal plating, but scorched hide, ash-matted fur, sinewy predator anatomy, coal eyes, smoke tails, and ember-orange cracks glowing beneath the skin. Reuse this look for later living beasts in the area when they should feel burned and hungry rather than armored or constructed.

Reference asset: `assets/opponents/ash-wastelands-02-cinder-hound.png`

Cindermaw is the mid-boss cousin of that family, but should read as a molten lion rather than a larger hound: a heavy feline chest, ash-fur mane, ember-lit jaws, and a commanding silhouette, still living ash-hide rather than armor or glass.

Reference asset: `assets/opponents/ash-wastelands-05-cindermaw.png`

Glassback Beetles define brittle volcanic-glass armor creatures: low heavy bodies, burnt chitin undersides, translucent obsidian and amber shell plates, and glowing seams beneath the shell. They should feel grown from cooled lava and glass, distinct from forged armor and living ash-hide beasts.

Reference asset: `assets/opponents/ash-wastelands-03-glassback-beetle.png`

Ember Wraiths define the area's spectral caster look: drifting ash-robes, hollow faces, ember cores, smoke-torn edges, and partial insubstantiality. They should feel like burned spirits, not physical ash-hide beasts or armored servants.

Reference asset: `assets/opponents/ash-wastelands-06-ember-wraiths.png`
